Email Signature Generator

Fill in your details, then copy the signature and paste it into your email client's signature settings.

Paste the public link to your org's logo (hosted on your website, Google Drive with public sharing, or an image host). It must be a direct link ending in an image, not a page link.
How to install this in Gmail:
  1. Fill in your name, title, phone, and email above (the logo and website are already filled in for you — no need to touch those).
  2. Click the "Copy signature" button below. You'll see a message confirming it copied.
  3. Open Gmail in your web browser, click the gear icon (⚙️) in the top right, then click "See all settings."
  4. You'll land on the "General" tab. Scroll down until you see the "Signature" section.
  5. Click "Create new," give it a name (like your own name), then click a spot inside the empty signature box.
  6. Paste with Ctrl+V (Windows) or Cmd+V (Mac). Your signature should appear fully formatted, with the logo and your info.
  7. Scroll down a little further to "Signature defaults" and set your new signature as the default for both "For new emails" and "On reply/forward."
  8. Scroll all the way down and click the blue "Save Changes" button. If you skip this last step, nothing will save!
  9. Test it: click Compose to start a new email and confirm your signature shows up automatically.
Troubleshooting:
  1. If the logo doesn't appear: give it a few seconds and refresh the compose window — it sometimes needs a moment to load the first time.
  2. If pasting shows plain text with no logo or formatting: make sure you clicked inside the signature box first, then use Ctrl+V / Cmd+V (not "Paste and match style," which strips formatting).
  3. If you're on a Mac and Cmd+V doesn't paste the formatted version, try right-clicking inside the box and choosing "Paste."
Using Outlook instead?
  1. Go to Settings → Mail → Compose and reply → Signature, then paste into the box the same way.
